A Tribute to Derek Jeter

You don't just accidentally show up in the World Series.

TIMELINE

1992: All-USA Today High School Baseball Player of the Year Award.

1994: Minor League Player of the Year Award.

1995: Derek Jeter joined the New York Yankees. Jeter appeared in 15 games in the season, had 48 at-bats, drove in 7 runs, scored 5 times.

1996: New York Yankees become the Major League Baseball World Series Champions. Outstanding Rookie of the Year Award (Player's Choice). Rookie of the Year Award (Baseball America). Rookie of the Year Award (BBWAA).

1998: New York Yankees become the Major League Baseball World Series Champions. MLB Player of the Month Award.

1999: New York Yankees become the Major League Baseball World Series Champions.

2000: New York Yankees become the Major League Baseball World Series Champions. All-Star Game Most Valuable Player Award. The Babe Ruth Award (The World Series Most Valuable Player). World Series Most Valuable Player Award.

2004: Gold Glove Award (Shortstop). MLB Player of the Week Award.

2005: Gold Glove Award (Shortstop).

2006: Baseball Digest Player of the Year Award. Gold Glove Award (Shortstop). Hank Aaron Award. Hitter of the Year Award. The Silver Slugger Award (American League).

2007: The Silver Slugger Award (American League).

2008: The Silver Slugger Award (American League).

2009: Gold Glove Award (Shortstop). Hank Aaron Award. Roberto Clemente Award. Sports Illustrated Sportsman of the Year Award. The Silver Slugger Award (American League).

2010: Gold Glove Award (Shortstop). Lou Gehrig Memorial Award. Willie, Mickey and the Duke Award.

2012: The Silver Slugger Award (American League).

2014: Jeter retires being The Captain. Wearing only the pinstripes uniform Jeter appeared in 145 games, had 581 at-bats, drove in 50 runs and scored 47 times. Commissioner's Historic Achievement Award.
